SNAKE

A push-your-luck card game

3 to 6 players · one deck · prod v1

A snake grows down the middle of the table. Everyone feeds it, nobody wants to be the one who overfeeds it. Feed it too far and it bites. Get bitten too often and the snake takes you out.

The goal

Don't get eaten

Every card you play feeds the snake and makes it a little longer. The snake has a maximum length. Push it past that and it bites whoever was holding the card. A bite costs you points, and in this game points are bad. They are snakebite.

The first player to reach 100 points has been finished off by the snake. They lose. Everyone still standing wins together. So the whole game is one long game of not-it.

What you need

One deck, three to six friends

A standard deck with both jokers left in, 54 cards. Three to six players. Something to keep score on, a pen and paper is plenty.

Pick a dealer for the first round. After every round the deal passes one seat to the left. Play goes around to the left until something changes it.

The cards

A turn

Play one card, say the number

You start with 4 cards. On your turn you play exactly one of them, feeding the snake or pulling a trick, and then you say the snake's new length out loud so the table can hear it.

You do not draw after every card. You play your hand all the way down, and only when it is empty do you draw a fresh 4. So everyone can watch your options shrink from four cards to one, which tells them when you are about to be in trouble.

There are only two things you are not allowed to do:

Rule one no overshoot

You cannot play a card that pushes the snake over its maximum length.

Rule two no trick last

You cannot play a trick (K, Q, J, A or joker) as the last card in your hand. Always keep a number card for the bottom.

If a trick is somehow the only card you have left, draw one card and play that instead if you can. A lucky pull there can even win you the round on the spot.

Ending a round

The bite or the pin

A round always ends one of two ways, and they could not be more different.

The Bite you lose the round

It is your turn and you are cornered. Every card you could play would shove the snake over the top, and you have no trick to wriggle out. The snake bites you. Take 10 points.

The Pin you win the round

You land the snake on its maximum exactly. You have pinned it. You score 0, everyone else takes 5. This is what the wild Ace is for.

The maximum length

Fifteen times the table

Count the heads at the table and multiply by fifteen. That is how long the snake can get before it bites. Easy to work out, even a few drinks in.

To start a round, flip the top card and use its number as the snake's starting length. If you flip a trick, set it aside and flip again until you get a number.

Call the length

Say it or lose it

Every time you play, you say the snake's new total out loud. This is harder than it sounds, because the Queen halves the snake and the Ace can be any value, so you cannot just count the cards on the table. You have to keep the real number in your head.

If it is your turn and you cannot say the length, you draw a card and the question passes back to the player before you. If nobody at the table can remember it, the snake resets to 10 and play carries on. Mumble at your own risk.

Example · the bite

Cornered with nowhere to go

SnakeSitting at 41. The max is 45, so only 4 of room is left.41

It is your turn and your hand is a 6, a 7 and a 9, with no trick to save you. Every card overshoots: 41 and 6 is 47, the 7 makes 48, the 9 makes 50. Nothing fits and you cannot wriggle out.

The snake bites you. Take 10 points and the round is over.

Example · the pin

Threading the needle

SnakeAt 38, with 7 of room left to the max of 45.38

You are holding an Ace and a 3. You play the Ace and call it a 7. 38 and 7 lands on 45 on the nose.

Pinned. You score 0, and everyone else takes 5. The cleanest win in the game.

Example · the spike and the counter

An Ace as a weapon

YouSnake is at 32. You play an Ace as a 7 to shove it up at your neighbour.39

At 39 the next player is in deep water. But the attack is a gamble on what they are holding. If they have a Queen, they shed the snake back to 19 and your whole spike melts away. If they have a King, they reverse the table and the 39 comes straight back at you.

Aggression near the top is a bet on the table, never a sure thing.

Example · the joker

A pure bully move

The snake is creeping high and the player after you is sitting pretty, hoarding two Queens to save themselves later. You play a Joker.

They throw away their entire hand, Queens and all, and draw four total strangers off the deck.

Maybe the new hand is better. Probably it is not. Nobody knows until they look, and that is exactly the point.
